The price of food:
what we can do

There are many solutions to the problem of increasing prices among crops and food in Northern California, although some of these will be easier than others. Among the many solutions include allowing the use of more GMOâ€™s as well as allowing an increased amount of water to be given to agriculture.

If farmers are allowed to use genetically modified crops, the price of said crops will dramatically decreases. This is due to the fact that genetically modified crops utilize less resources than organic crops and this, in turn, will lead to the crops taking up less fertilizer, pesticides, as well as water, which all cost money. This decrease in the need for resources will cause the price to go down as well. However, this solution comes with a drawback, the main one being that genetically modified organisms have a stigma attached to them, and some people believe that they are less healthy than organic crops.

A second solution to the problem of expensive crops would be to allow the farms that produce the crops a bigger share of the water. By allocating a larger proportion of the water to agricultural use, it will allow farmers to use more water when watering their crops and therefore produce a higher quality of food for the market. Again, this solution comes with a downside. Mainly being that by distributing more water to farms and agricultural purposes, less water will be allocated to urban and residential areas.
